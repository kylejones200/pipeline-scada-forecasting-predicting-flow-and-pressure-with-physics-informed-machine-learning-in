When TransCanada's pipeline control system failed to predict pressure surges during rapid demand changes in 2019, the resulting overpressure event triggered emergency shutdowns across three compressor stations, costing $8 million in lost throughput and regulatory penalties.

This tutorial walks through  pipeline SCADA forecasting using temporal lag feature engineering capturing 1-24 hour autocorrelation, Gaussian Process models for smooth flow predictions with uncertainty quantification, Random Forest for non-linear pressure dynamics, and physics-informed constraint enforcement ensuring hydraulic consistency (Ps > Pd always).

This matters because traditional SCADA alarm systems react to violations rather than preventing them through forecasting. The difference between reacting to a pressure alarm versus preventing it through 12-hour-ahead forecasting can mean the difference between smooth operations and expensive emergency responses that disrupt customer deliveries.
